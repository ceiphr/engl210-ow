Trials and Imprisonment of Oscar Wilde

What This Project Encompasses:

This project is intended to be a website which covers key events and people within the Trials of Oscar Wilde. In the navigation bar you will find a link to the timeline and profiles pages. The timeline page illustrates key events that are directly and indirectly connected to the trials. The profiles page is intended to display pseudo social media profiles for the major people involved in the trials. The website source code is the HTML and CSS which was hand coded for this project’s website. The work cited is an MLA formatted document containing all the sources to facts and media assets used in this project. I hope you enjoy what we’ve made.

The Trials in A Nutshell:

Oscar Wilde is involved in a romantic relationship with Sir Alfred Douglas during the time of Wilde’s opening play, “The Importance of Being Earnest”. Alfred’s father the Marquess of Queensberry is disgusted by his son’s behavior and tries to tarnish Wilde’s reputation by sending him a card making claims that he is a “sodomite”. This leads to Wilde bringing charges against Alfred's father in the Queensbury trials, which instead results in incriminating Wilde for having relations with young men. Shortly after an official trail is brought against Oscar Wilde for homosexuality and 25 counts of gross indecency. He pleaded not guilty. After many months and three separate trials. Wilde was found guilty and sentenced to two years of hard labor. After completing his sentence he fled to France to live out the rest of his years with Alfred Douglas.
